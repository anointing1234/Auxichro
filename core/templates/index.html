About Us | Chronic Disease Mate for Africa

Chronic Disease Mate for Africa (CDMA)

Chronic Disease Mate for Africa (CDMA) is a pioneering umbrella organization dedicated to addressing the growing burden of non-communicable diseases (NCDs) across the African continent. With a mission to reduce premature mortality and improve quality of life, CDMA collaborates with healthcare providers, communities, and policymakers to promote integrated, patient-centered care for chronic conditions such as cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, cancer, and chronic respiratory diseases.

Founded to tackle the rising prevalence of NCDs, which account for an increasing share of mortality in sub-Saharan Africa, CDMA supports innovative strategies like the WHO’s PEN-Plus initiative, focusing on early diagnosis, treatment, and community-based care. By fostering partnerships, advocating for policy development, and leveraging technology such as mHealth solutions, CDMA empowers African health systems to address both infectious and non-communicable diseases effectively.
